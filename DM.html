Doctor of Management (DM)

Lordland University, USA

Program Overview

The Doctor of Management (DM) at Lordland University is designed for senior executives, managers, and professionals who aim to integrate business expertise, scientific methodologies, and Christian ethical principles into their leadership practices. This program focuses on strategic management, innovation, global leadership, and ethical decision-making, preparing graduates to lead organizations with integrity and vision.

Rooted in a faith-based educational approach, the DM program aligns scientific problem-solving with Christian leadership principles, ensuring that graduates lead with wisdom, servant leadership, and ethical responsibility in business, government, and nonprofit sectors.

Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

  1. Conduct High-Level Research: Contribute to business and management scholarship.
  2. Develop Advanced Strategies: Create strategies for organizational growth and transformation.
  3. Apply Contemporary Theories: Use modern business theories to tackle real-world challenges.
  4. Lead Global Operations: Manage complex business operations on a global scale.
  5. Publish Impactful Research: Produce research in peer-reviewed journals and industry publications.
  6. Apply Ethical Standards: Uphold professional and ethical standards in research and practice.

DM Curriculum Structure

The DM curriculum consists of:

  • Core Courses (18 credits): 6 courses
  • Executive Leadership & Strategy (18 credits): 6 courses
  • Dissertation Research (6 credits): 6 phases

Total: 18 courses, 42 credits.

Conclusion

The Doctor of Management (DM) at Lordland University prepares faith-driven business leaders to tackle global challenges while maintaining ethical integrity and Christian values. By integrating scientific methodologies, business expertise, and faith, graduates are equipped to lead organizations with wisdom, vision, and a commitment to positive societal impact.

Graduation Requirements

  • Complete 42 credits of coursework and research.
  • Maintain a minimum GPA of 3.0 on a 4.0 scale.
  • Successfully complete all coursework and dissertation requirements.
  • Defend the final dissertation before an academic panel.
