Master of Science in Computer Science Engineering (MSCSE)

Lordland University, USA

Program Overview

The Master of Science in Computer Science Engineering (MSCSE) at Lordland University is designed to provide students with advanced technical knowledge, business acumen, and ethical leadership rooted in Christian values. This program integrates cutting-edge technology in robotics, drone technology, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and data analytics with business strategies and ethical computing practices.

With a focus on robotics and drone technology, the program prepares students to develop autonomous systems, intelligent machines, and UAV applications that are revolutionizing industries such as agriculture, defense, logistics, healthcare, and urban development.

Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

  1. Apply Advanced Computing & Robotics Principles: Design intelligent systems, robotic algorithms, and AI-driven automation for real-world applications.
  2. Integrate Business & Technology Strategies: Utilize computer science, drone technology, and AI to enhance business operations and strategic planning.
  3. Develop AI, Robotics & UAV Systems: Create autonomous drones, self-learning robots, and robotic process automation solutions.
  4. Enhance Cybersecurity & Data Privacy: Implement secure network protocols and ethical AI practices in emerging technologies.
  5. Master Cloud, IoT & Big Data Analytics: Deploy high-performance computing solutions leveraging cloud and edge computing.
  6. Apply Christian Ethics in Technology: Demonstrate faith-driven leadership by aligning technological advancements with moral values.
  7. Advance Computational Research & Innovation: Conduct scientific research in robotics, automation, and drone technology.
  8. Communicate Effectively in Teams: Collaborate in interdisciplinary teams to solve complex engineering challenges.

Program Structure

The MSCSE curriculum consists of:

  • Core Curriculum (32 Credits): 8 courses
  • Specialization Courses (24 Credits): 6 courses
  • Capstone Project (4 Credits): 1 course
  • Total: 15 courses, 60 credits

Specialization Tracks (24 Credits)

Students choose 6 courses from one or more of the following tracks:

  • Robotics & Drone Technology Track: MSCSE620, MSCSE625, MSCSE630, MSCSE635, MSCSE640, MSCSE645.
  • Cybersecurity & Cloud Computing Track: MSCSE650, MSCSE655, MSCSE660, MSCSE665.
  • Software Development & AI Track: MSCSE670, MSCSE675, MSCSE680, MSCSE685.
  • Data Science & Analytics Track: MSCSE690, MSCSE695, MSCSE700, MSCSE705.

Faith-Based Leadership in Technology

  • Christian Approach to Tech Leadership: Integrating faith-driven decision-making, ethical AI, and responsible computing into the curriculum.
  • Technology as a Tool for Good: Ensuring advancements in robotics, drones, AI, and cybersecurity align with Christian morals and values.
  • Servant Leadership in Tech: Leading with humility, integrity, and ethical responsibility based on Biblical principles.
  • Ethical AI & Robotics: Upholding human dignity in automation, AI decision-making, and UAV applications.
  • Digital Transformation & Global Stewardship: Using technology to improve lives, bridge digital divides, and promote social good worldwide.

  • Business & Leadership Integration: Develop strategic thinking and ethical leadership alongside technical expertise.

Conclusion

The Master of Science in Computer Science Engineering (MSCSE) at Lordland University prepares graduates to become innovative, ethical, and faith-driven leaders in robotics, AI, cybersecurity, and drone technology. By combining advanced engineering skills, business strategy, and Christian leadership, students will drive technological advancements while upholding moral integrity.
