Doctor of Public Health (DrPH)

Lordland University, USA

Program Overview

The Doctor of Public Health (DrPH) at Lordland University, USA, is a professional doctorate designed to develop high-level public health leaders, policymakers, researchers, and educators. This program integrates advanced scientific research, healthcare management, public health policy, safety, business strategies, and Christian ethics, equipping graduates to tackle complex health challenges at local, national, and global levels.

This 3 to 4-year program follows a quarter system with 12 credit hours per quarter, totaling 96 credit hours. Students engage in advanced coursework, leadership training, applied research, and a dissertation project that contributes to public health innovation.

Program Structure

The DrPH curriculum consists of:

  • Core Courses (60 credits): Advanced public health leadership and research.
  • Elective Courses (18 credits): Specialization in business, data analytics, faith-based healthcare, or epidemiology.
  • Dissertation & Practicum (18 credits): Independent research and field experience.

Students can complete the program in 3 to 4 years, depending on their study pace (full-time or part-time).
