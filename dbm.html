Doctor of Business and Management (DBM)

Lordland University

Program Overview

The Doctor of Business and Management (DBM) program at Lordland University is designed for senior professionals, executives, and scholars seeking advanced knowledge in business strategies, organizational leadership, and global market trends. This research-focused program integrates theoretical frameworks with practical applications, fostering innovation, strategic decision-making, and leadership excellence.

Program Objectives

  1. Conduct High-Level Research: Contribute to business and management scholarship.
  2. Develop Advanced Strategies: Create strategies for organizational growth and transformation.
  3. Apply Contemporary Theories: Use modern business theories to tackle real-world challenges.
  4. Lead Global Operations: Manage complex business operations on a global scale.
  5. Publish Impactful Research: Produce research in peer-reviewed journals and industry publications.

Admission Requirements

  • A Master’s degree in Business, Management, or a related field from an accredited institution.
  • A minimum GPA of 3.5 on a 4.0 scale.
  • Statement of purpose outlining research interests and career goals.
  • Three letters of recommendation from academic or industry professionals.
  • Proof of English proficiency (for non-native speakers).

The DBM program consists of 60 credit hours, including coursework, research, and dissertation work.

Graduation Requirements

  • Complete 60 credit hours of coursework and research.
  • Maintain a minimum GPA of 3.5.
  • Successfully defend a doctoral dissertation.
  • Publish at least one research paper in a peer-reviewed journal.
  • Complete the program within a maximum of 6 years.
